Masashi Kishimoto

Japanese manga artist

  • 1995- Kishimoto's first successful manga pilot was Karakuri (カラクリ, lit. "Mechanism"), which he submitted to Shueisha
  • 1996 - earned him an honorable mention in Shueisha's monthly "Hop Step Award", = granted to promising rookie manga artists.
  • 1997 - he wrote a one-shot version of Naruto which was published in Akamaru Jump Summer.
  • 1999 - the serialized version of Naruto premiered in Weekly Shōnen Jump 1999 No. 43 and quickly became a hit.
  • 2006 - The seventh volume of Viz's release became the first manga to ever win a Quill Award when it claimed the award for "Best Graphic Novel"
  • 2009 - For the video game Tekken 6, Kishimoto redesigned its new character, Lars Alexandersson.
  • 2010 - Kishimoto produced a one-shot baseball manga, Bench, as part of Jump's "Top of the Super Legend" project, a series of six one-shot manga by famed Weekly Shōnen Jump artists.
  • 2012 - it was announced that Kishimoto would publish a one-shot version of his long-postponed mafia manga, Mario, in Jump Square,based on the rough, 160-page manuscript he began working on before Naruto became serialized.
  • 2013 - several of Kishimoto's one-shots saw their English-language debut in issues of the Weekly Shonen Jump digital magazine, including Mario, Bench, and the original Naruto pilot.
  • 2014 - Naruto ended on November 10,2014 which made Kishimoto the winner of "Rookie of the Year" for the series in the Agency for Cultural Affairs. It was adapted into two successful anime series, Naruto and Naruto Shippuden.
  • 2015 - Kishimoto also illustrated the cover of violinist Chisako Takashima's album Strings on Fire.
  • 2017 - it was confirmed that Kishimoto was developing a new science fiction adventure series tentatively scheduled to debut in 2018.
  • 2018 - the series was formally announced as Samurai 8: The Tale of Hachimaru.Kishimoto will be handling the script and rough storyboards, while Akira Ōkubo, a former assistant on Naruto and brother of Atsushi Ōkubo, is responsible for illustrating the final manuscript.
